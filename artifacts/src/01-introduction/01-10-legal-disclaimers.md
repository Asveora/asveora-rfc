## 1.10.5 Identity and Liability Separation

The Asveora protocol defines identity structures (**PIK**, **VSIG**, **APA**, **AUID**) strictly as technical constructs. These constructs do NOT represent legal identity verification, constitute legal identity authentication, imply real-world identity validation, or establish liability or legal accountability relationships. Any mapping between Asveora identity systems and real-world identity systems is implementation-specific and outside the scope of this RFC.

## 1.10.7 Federation Independence

Each Network within the Asveora ecosystem operates independently. Accordingly, no Network is legally responsible for another Network. Federation participation does NOT imply legal partnership or liability sharing. Data synchronization between instances does NOT establish legal control relationships and governance decisions are locally scoped unless explicitly defined otherwise. Federation is a technical interoperability model, not a legal organizational structure.
