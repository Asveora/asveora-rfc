I. The Preamble of Asveora
Asveora is a digital network that operates on the model of a Decentralized Democratic Constitutional Republic (DDCR) and serves with the purpose of helping people become more technologically self-sustainable and independent while also combatting the faults of centralized social media and Late Stage Web 2. It has been built with the intent to generate authentic connections and discussions while cutting out all of the white noise and pandemonium that has become so common elsewhere over the internet. This constitution that you are now reading is the official Terms of Service (ToS) of the Asveora network (also known as the asveora.social network). It applies to your access to and use of Asveora and any virtual properties directly related to it. This constitution was drafted by the first declared federation of Asveora known as “Asveora Genesis”. While this constitution is subject to amendment by the inalienable political power of the populace of Asveora, some guidelines, statutes, and directives cannot be amended if they exist to comply with national or international laws.

IV. Privacy & Security
Asveora is committed to ensuring privacy and security through transparency and education. Asveora does not collect, use, nor share the information about you when you access or use its network and it severely discourages anyone attempting to do such. There is no long-winded privacy policy here to read but instead here are some crucial notes about security and safety in Asveora and strategies you can employ across the internet.

01. Create strong passwords for the means by which you will access Asveora. Use a strong mix of characters and symbols that will not be easily brute forced or guessable by malicious figures. The more complex and unique your password is, the better.

02. Do not share your passwords with anyone else aside from yourself and yourself alone.

03. Make sure that your passwords are stored securely inside of an encrypted file on your choice of digital hardware or service. Never store your passwords in your e-mail, a personal diary, or in other insecure locations.

04. Never give out your e-mail address, phone number, home address, and other sensitive information.

05. Read the terms and conditions of every tool and service that is external to Asveora. Read their policies to ensure that they are respecting your privacy and security in full.

06. Never enter your financial or personal information on a website that is not secure. Always look for “https://” and a padlock icon in your web browser’s address bar. And even then, you may be required to vet the website yourself to make sure that it is not a phishing pond disguised as a secure website.

07. Never click suspicious web links or shortened web links and do not accept cookies from websites that you are not familiar with. Always run shortened or sketchy web links through trusted link checking services or use the built in developer tools of your web browser to inspect the link for anything out of the ordinary.

08. Do not share your IP address, MAC address, or device ID with anyone.

09. If you suspect that your financial information has been stolen, immediately contact your bank.

10. If you suspect that your IP address has been leaked in some form, immediately obtain a new one.

11. Do not ever trust insecure public WiFi networks. Always make sure they are secured in some form.

12. Be conscious of and actively manage your personal data wherever you go.

13. Enable multi-factor authentication whenever it is available.

14. Use a reputable VPN service to keep your IP address safe and sound.

15. Never provide links to sources where your personal information may be visible. Always disclose the privacy and security policies of the links to materials and sites that you may share.

16. No one from Asveora will ever ask you for your e-mail address, phone number, home address, device serial number, IP address, MAC address, credit card, driver’s license, or any other form of personal or critical data. There are no official offices or employees of Asveora, so if anyone contacts you posing as an employee of Asveora and asks for sensitive information, immediately report them to the support and assistance channels of whatever wheelcore, landhub, system, and or federation that you are a part of. If anyone asks for your sensitive information or is devising ways to aggregate data of you and others within the network, be sure to report them as well. Asveora is dedicated to security, trust, and integrity.

VIII. Democratic Rights and Network Removals
Asveora is built upon the model of a Decentralized Democratic Constitutional Republic (DDCR). This means that the executive direction is determined through the political discourse and voting abilities of the members of Asveora whether it be in a wheelcore, landhub, system, or a federation. Asveorans have the right to vote on all polled issues within Asveora as this is a part of their democratic rights. External Visitors on the other hand cannot vote since they are not members / citizens of Asveora. No person or entity within Asveora can cast a vote on behalf of a person or entity outside of Asveora.

If an issue arises within Asveora such as ToS violations or other kinds of misbehavior, an Asveoran may file a complaint in the Stoat server of the Asveora Network Plaza community. Depending on the issue, they may also be recommended to report the issue to the platform provider or server host. Filing a complaint will trigger a court case. Every Asveoran has a right to due process and the proceedings for any administrative discipline will follow the outcome of a court case as these cases are held to determine the resolution to an issue.

Asveorans are required to report to the Asveora Network Plaza community’s Stoat server if they are summoned to Asveora’s court. Being summoned to the court (also known as a court summons) is a mandatory directive and the Asveoran that is being summoned must appear within the provided time frame declared by the court summon. This still applies even if they primarily reside on another platform and protocol different from Stoat. Asveorans that do not appear for their court summon will be FTA (Failure to Appear) and given a strict countdown period before their FTA status converts into automatic disciplinary action which may result in a network ban.

Court cases are held to ensure the fair treatment of every Asveoran and an honest assessment of the raised issue. They include the participation of every assembly within Asveora. Court case outcomes are determined by the review of a composite jury to ensure that rulings are truly democratic in execution. Depending on the severity of the violation, the case might be expedited at the direction of the Administrative and Executive assemblies to ensure network safety and security.

The governing matters of Asveora are not to be conflated nor confused with the legal systems of real world countries. For instance, if a user breaches their agreement to the constitution of Asveora but breaks no laws of their country or international laws, they may be removed from Asveora but may not be in real life legal trouble. If a user breaches their agreement to the constitution of Asveora which involves them breaking the laws of their country or international laws, they may be removed from Asveora and subsequently reported to the appropriate agencies.

The democratic right to vote may be revoked from an Asveoran if they are on trial within the network. Network removals are a highly unlikely occurrence as they are decided by network wide review. This is being noted here because if you are removed from Asveora, you might have more pressing problems on your hands to worry about than just being removed from the network. Just as with other administrative actions, network removals can be appealed, but the process may take a substantial amount of time. However you most likely will never have to worry about such happening if you follow the laws of your country and the constitution of Asveora. Network removals also does not mean you cannot deploy Asveora independently since the project is open source. It just means you will be removed from the asveora.social ecosystem and barred from participating.

External Visitors cannot be summoned under any circumstance since they do not exist as a member / citizen of the Asveora network. Therefore Asveora does not govern them in any direct manner. If an External Visitor violates the ToS of Asveora, they must be dealt with directly by the Asveoran that is operating the space where External Visitor access has been enabled. If an External Visitor violates the ToS of the instance or platform that they are using, then they must be reported to the appropriate departments of that instance or platform that have been tasked with handling trust and safety.

XI. Accessing Matrix and Staying Safe
You are prohibited from accessing the public Matrix ecosystem with your Asveora account. If you would like to access the public Matrix ecosystem then please create a separate Matrix account for that purpose. Matrix is an amazing protocol with a massive decentralized community of its own, but the protocol has been abused by bad actors due to its decentralized nature. To ensure your safety it is mandated that you only interact with members of Asveora and Asveoran communities on Matrix. Any unverified or external entities should be promptly reported and removed from spaces or rooms. When in doubt, always check the usernames database or consult the administration of Asveora.

Occasionally you may be contacted through direct messaging rooms by the host of a homeserver that you are using with information regarding updates about the status, security, and upgrades of a homeserver. You do not need to respond to these. If you end up having any problems with your Matrix account on a certain homeserver, directly email the host of the homeserver (their contact information can usually be found at the homeserver's root domain) rather than processing the issue directly through the Matrix protocol.

NEVER accept direct messaging room invites from accounts that you have not interacted with in a common setting somewhere within the Asveora network. If you are contacted with content that is prohibited under rule XIV then immediately report the account to the Asveora administration through a support channel on Stoat and flag the content for the host of the homeserver you're using by using the report function within your Matrix client.

No one from the Asveora administration will ever ask for your name, phone number, credit card, street address, ID, or any kind of identifying information. If someone reaches out asking for sensitive information or posing as a member of Asveora's administration then immediately report the behavior and block them. Always ask in a support channel if you are unsure or have concerns about an account that you have come across.
XII. Transfer and Backup of Communications Profile
In the case of the matrix protocol and similar chat protocols, Asveorans may transfer homeservers or create backup presences on other homeservers as long as they employ their verified APC (Asveora Profile Code) which will serve as the key for re-authentication when adding backup presences into the network or making transfers. Asveorans may not pair an APC with more than one APA (Asveora Profile Address) as it will may risk invalidating their AUID (Asveora User ID) or causing conflicts in the network’s logs. When a homeserver transfer is happening or a backup is being created, the Asveoran must show proof of authorship in the scanning process by adding the homeserver details to their profile space for the designated period of time as per given instruction.

XVI. Buying & Selling in the Services Marketplace
All buyers, sellers, and other participants within the services marketplace of asveora.social must be members of the network. If you are not a member of the network you cannot purchase services, sell services, or participate in any kind of transaction involving Asveora’s services marketplace. This is to ensure that all transactions are properly logged within the network. All transactions utilizing Asveora’s services marketplace MUST be reported through the appropriate channel within the Stoat server of the Asveora Network Plaza community. If you decide to operate outside of Asveora’s services marketplace, Asveora is not responsible for any damages that may incur.

The asveora.social network and Asveora project do not make money from transactions, process transactions, or offer protections on transactions. Asveora will intervene with a network ban if fraud is discovered in order to ensure the integrity of its marketplace. However the marketplace strictly acts as a search engine and operates operates under Section 230 of USA law.

This means that it cannot be legally held liable for the offerings of a 3rd party. All sellers within the services marketplace are considered 3rd party entities and freelancers. They are not employed by Asveora or associated officially with the organization in any capacity. Asveora does not sell, offer for sale, or direct the sale of any services. It simply provides a search engine for independent parties who are offering services to the public (fellow Asveorans). If you come across a listing or seller that is encouraging the sale of fraudulent services, please immediately report it through the support center if you are not within the network. If you are within the network then please report it both through the support center and the support channel of the Asveora Network Plaza Stoat server.

Fraud is taken extremely seriously as it damages trust in the marketplace and hurts people. Fraud victims will be redirected to the proper legal resources if they wish to pursue legal action from within their territory. Asveora is not a legal agency nor legal advisor and is not authorized to offer any professional legal advice in any official capacity. Please use the proper sources you are redirected to. In order to be added to the Asveora’s marketplace listings as a service provider you must complete the appropriate verification process that is established within the network for becoming a listed seller. All listings are recommendations and, as previous outlined, sellers are not officially associated with Asveora in any legal capacity. All sellers are responsible for determining the payment processor they will use (PayPal, Stripe, Wise, Square, etc.) and how the taxes are handled from what they make through freelancing.

Asveora does not process taxes or offer tools to assist sellers with doing so. If a seller chooses to use an untrustworthy or controversial payment processor, their listings may be removed from the marketplace. Cryptocurrency and virtual items are not allowed as a transaction solution and any seller requesting for cryptocurrencies, NFTs, blockchain items, or virtual assets such as video game skins as a form of payment will be removed from the market. This is enforced to ensure transparency and safety.

Advertisements and sponsorships must be disclosed as such in an explicit manner. Paywalls are entirely prohibited. You are free to encourage donations to support your work however. Links to external marketplaces are allowed but Asveora is not responsible for the products or services offered in those marketplaces and cannot be legally held liable for any damages incurred by using them. Asveora does not receive commissions, monetary gifts, or payouts from any of the external marketplaces that Asveorans may link to.
XVIII. Malicious Links, Malware, & Attacks
The sharing of malicious links, sharing of malware, or the execution of web or device attacks of any kind will result in a network removal. This includes, but not limited to, phishing, IP grabbers, fuzzing, ransomware, cryptojacking, man-in-the-middle attacks (MITM), cross-site scripting (XSS), cross-site request forgery (CSRF), SQL injection, and plenty of other malicious behaviors that seek to compromise the device, security, or identity of an individual, service, organization, or other entity.
XIX. AI Generated Content
The Asveora network (asveora.social) is committed to transparency, trust, and safety. AI generated content must be marked as such within the network. With an increasing volume of AI generated content circulating around on the internet as the months and years go on, Asveora wants to ensure that Asveorans know what content they are looking at and how that content came to be. No matter how you feel personally about AI, never attempt to pass off AI generated content as being generated or produced by other means. Honesty and integrity is highly valued within Asveora and we want to ensure that those values are carried through how media and content is shared.

AI generated content that is created to deceive, harm, or exploit individuals is entirely prohibited and will result in a ban from the network. This includes but is not limited to deepfakes, AI manufactured events, and AI editing processes that obscure the true contents or events of a piece of non-AI generated media. Parody creations utilizing technologies such as voice cloning may only be allowed if explicitly marked as being AI generated and a parody. These parodies must also not exist to harm, exploit, or deceive. If they do, the same penalties and restrictions outlined above will apply in full.
