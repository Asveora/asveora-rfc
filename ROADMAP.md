# Asveora Roadmap

This document outlines the intended evolution of the Asveora RFC ecosystem and supporting standards.

It reflects **conceptual direction**, not implementation commitments.

---

## Phase 1 — Core Specification Stabilization (Current)

Status: In Progress

Focus:
- Finalizing RFC structure and terminology
- Stabilizing identity model (CIR, PIK, APA, VSIG, AUID)
- Defining registry and commit systems
- Establishing federation and governance models
- Aligning schemas with RFC definitions

Outcome:
- A stable, versioned RFC v1 specification
- Clear structural foundation for interoperable systems

---

## Phase 2 — Human Accessibility Layer

Status: Planned

Focus:
- Creating a beginner-friendly “Asveora Concepts Handbook”
- Translating technical RFCs into accessible explanations
- Defining real-world analogies for identity, federation, and governance
- Improving onboarding clarity for new contributors

Outcome:
- A readable “entry layer” for non-technical participants
- Reduced complexity barrier for ecosystem adoption

---

## Phase 3 — Ecosystem Adoption & Reference Implementations

Status: Future

Focus:
- Reference implementations of Asveora-compatible systems
- Example network instances
- Tooling for validation and schema compliance
- Developer libraries and integration guides

Outcome:
- Real-world usable implementations of the RFC standard
- Verified interoperability across systems

---

## Phase 4 — Federation Expansion

Status: Future

Focus:
- Interconnection of independent Asveora instances
- Cross-instance identity portability
- Federation governance coordination models
- Trust and reconciliation mechanisms between networks

Outcome:
- A functioning federated ecosystem of sovereign networks

---

## Phase 5 — Governance Evolution

Status: Long-term

Focus:
- Expansion of distributed governance models
- Instance-level constitutional variation
- Federation-wide coordination frameworks
- Improved transparency and auditability mechanisms

Outcome:
- Mature decentralized governance ecosystem

---

## Design Philosophy

Across all phases, Asveora remains committed to:

- User sovereignty and identity ownership
- Decentralized and federated architecture
- Interoperability between independent systems
- Deterministic and auditable system behavior
- Separation of specification and implementation

---

## Note

This roadmap is intentionally flexible. It is intended to guide conceptual direction rather than enforce strict delivery timelines.
